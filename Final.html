Universal Design vs Accessible Design

Designing for Accessibility: focus on meeting a set of regulations / codes / standards
Universal Design: create a design for the widest number of users by anticipating interactions
Accessibility: usually achieved with a separate design or assistive technology product
- ex: screen readers, sign language interpreter

Universal design seeks to create one design for ALL users

Background

Sources indicate that about 61,000,000 people in the United States are living with some sort of disability. Thats about 18% of the population. Of the population, 91% of Americans use the internet. Universal Design aims to create laws and guidelines that decrease or eliminate barriers for accessing information online so that people with disabilities have the same access as everyone else. These guidelines are designed to help everyone by creating simple and intuitive navigation, legible fonts and colors, and other features that promote ease of access for people with visual, mobility, hearing, and learning disabilities but also the general public.
