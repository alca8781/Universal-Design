Universal Design vs Accessible Design

Designing for Accessibility: focus on meeting a set of regulations / codes / standards
Universal Design: create a design for the widest number of users by anticipating interactions
Accessibility: usually achieved with a separate design or assistive technology product
- ex: screen readers, sign language interpreter

Universal design seeks to create one design for ALL users
