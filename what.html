What is Universal Design

What is Universal Design?

Definition

Universal design refers to the laws and guidelines that developers follow to ensure equitable and simple use for all users.

Main Content

1. Equitable Use

The design is useful and marketable to people with diverse abilities
- high contrast
- text equivalents
- no mouse-only interactions

2. Flexible Use

The design accommodates a wide range of individual preferences and abilities
- no scroll-jacking: taking over how a user would expect scrolling to work
- allow for text resizing

3. Simple & Intuitive Use

Use of the design is easy to understand regardless of :
- knowledge
- language skills
- current concentration levels

4. Perceptible Information

The design communicates necessary information effectively to the user regardless of:
- ambient conditions
- sensory abilities

5. Tolerance for Error

The design minimizes hazards / unintended actions & consequences
- avoid accidents : use "are you sure" prompts
- allow user to undo actions

6. Low Physical Effort

The design can be used efficiently and comfortably with minimal fatigue
- action grouping : group actions to specific area of the screen
- minimize requests (tedious forms)

7. Size & Space For Approach & Use

Appropriate size and space is provided for approach, reach, manipulation & use regardless of:
- users size / posture / mobility
- dynamic spaces
- action targets : make large enough to click / tap easily
